Ghost Trick is a visual novel/puzzle style game released for the Nintendo DS and iOS, created by Shu Takumi, who is well known for being the creator of the Ace Attorney series. Takumi first started thinking about making Ghost Trick in 2004 for the GBA, when the Ace Attorney series were finished. Then the Nintendo DS came out and the decision was made to port the AA series over to the DS and so Ghost Trick had to wait until the ports were done.

The music is composed by Masakazu Sugimori, who also worked on the score for the first Ace Attorney game.

You start off the game as a ghost named Sissel who has just died. He wakes up to see a woman held at gunpoint and thinks he can't do anything about it because he's already dead. However, it turns out he has received the powers of the dead, "ghost tricks". With these ghost tricks, he is able to manipulate the environment around him and manages to save the woman from her fate. He then realises that he has lost his memory and sets out on a quest to find them, as well as solve the mystery behind the strange things that are happening in this town tonight.

Though Ghost Trick hasn't had huge sales on the DS, it has been well received for the most part by those who have played it. It was the second best-selling video game in Japan during its first week and was nominated for Best DS Game of E3 2010 by GameTrailers, though it lost to Okamiden. The game has a metascore of 83. It is generally praised for the story, unique gameplay and the characters.
